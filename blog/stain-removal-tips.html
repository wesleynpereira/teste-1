Stain Removal 101: What Works (and What Makes Stains Worse)

Not every stain responds to the same treatment, and the wrong first move can set a stain permanently before a professional ever sees it. A few rules worth following the moment a spill happens:

  • Blot, don't rub. Rubbing pushes the stain deeper into the fibers and spreads it outward.
  • Skip the hot water on protein-based stains like blood or dairy, which can cook the stain into the fiber.
  • Avoid mixing cleaning products. Combining chemicals can react badly with both the carpet and each other.
  • Don't scrub hard with a brush. It can fray fibers and create a worn-looking patch.

If a stain has already dried, or it's an older spot that's resisted at-home treatment, professional stain removal uses targeted, stain-specific methods instead of a one-size-fits-all spray.

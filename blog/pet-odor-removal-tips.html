Pet Odor Removal Tips for Carpets and Upholstery

Pet odor is rarely just a surface problem. Once urine or accidents soak into carpet padding or upholstery fill, regular vacuuming and store-bought sprays only mask the smell instead of removing it.

A few things that genuinely help between professional visits:

  • Blot fresh accidents immediately instead of rubbing them in
  • Use an enzyme-based cleaner, not a fragrance-based one, on pet stains
  • Run a fan or open a window to keep the area dry, since odor-causing bacteria thrive in damp fibers

If the smell comes back after cleaning the surface, or you can't pinpoint exactly where it's coming from, that's usually a sign the odor has reached the carpet padding or furniture fill. At that point, DIY treatments stop being effective.

Why Area Rugs Need a Different Cleaning Approach Than Wall-to-Wall Carpet

It's tempting to treat an area rug like an extension of your wall-to-wall carpet, but the two need different care. Area rugs are often wool, antique, or hand-dyed, which means the wrong cleaning method can cause color bleed, shrinkage, or fringe damage that wall-to-wall carpet simply doesn't risk in the same way.

Before any cleaning begins, a rug should be checked for:

  • Fiber type, since wool and synthetic blends respond differently to moisture
  • Dye stability, to avoid colors bleeding into each other
  • Fringe and edge condition, which need gentle hand cleaning rather than machine extraction

Homeowners in Ridgefield, New Canaan, and Wilton often bring in entry rugs, accent rugs, and family heirloom rugs for cleaning that wall-to-wall carpet cleaning alone won't address properly.

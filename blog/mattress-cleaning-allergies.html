Mattress Cleaning and Sanitizing: Why It Matters for Allergies

A mattress sits under a sleeper for roughly eight hours a night, every night, quietly collecting dust, dead skin cells, and the allergens that feed dust mites. Regular vacuuming barely touches what builds up below the surface.

For households dealing with allergies, asthma, or general sensitivity to dust, mattress cleaning and sanitizing can make a real difference. The process typically includes:

  • Deep vacuuming to lift dust and allergen buildup
  • A sanitizing pass across the full mattress surface
  • Targeted treatment for visible stains or odor
  • A fast-dry process so the mattress is usable again quickly

Many homeowners in Trumbull, Shelton, and Monroe schedule mattress cleaning once or twice a year, often alongside a bedroom carpet cleaning for a full refresh of the room.
